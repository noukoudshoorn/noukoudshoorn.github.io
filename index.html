Currently, I am a PhD candidate in the Knowledge-Based Systems group at the Technical University of Vienna, under the supervision of Magdalena Ortiz. Our research mainly focusses on constructing mathematical foundations for the SHACL standard, as presented by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). In particular, we are working on adding implicit knowledge in the form of ontologies to SHACL validation. For this, we work on description logics, knowledge representation, query answering and more. This project is part of the LogiCS-COFUND doctoral programme. Furthermore, I am interested in, among others, automata theory, proof theory, fixed point logics and the history of philosophy.

Sustainability

Travelling to conferences, workshops and other research institutions is very important to foster international collaborations. However, we only have one world, one that we should take care of. Therefore, to make a statement and because I like to travel by train, I use public transport (and avoid planes) for my travels whenever I can. Some examples of long international train journeys are:

  • January 2023: Vienna (Austria) - Umeå (Sweden) - Nijmegen (the Netherlands) - Vienna (Austria).
  • March/April 2023: Vienna (Austria) - Umeå (Sweden) - Göteborg (Sweden) - Nijmegen (The Netherlands) - Vienna (Austria).
  • July 2023: Stockholm (Sweden) - Umeå (Sweden) - Nijmegen (the Netherlands) - Vienna (Austria) (trains to enter Sweden where fully booked because of the summer season).
  • June 2024 (upcoming): Dresden (Germany) - Bergen (Norway) - Göteborg (Sweden) - Dresden (Germany).

By booking the trips early, buying some 25% or 50% discount cards in countries on the route or considering to buy an interrail pass, costs do not have to be so high.
